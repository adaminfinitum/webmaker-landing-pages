This October, 1 million Americans will celebrate Lights On Afterschool to rally support for the afterschool programs that keep kids safe, inspire them to learn and help working families. Mozilla and the Afterschool Alliance want to help you celebrate with a simple, fun activity to help build students’ webmaking and digital literacy skills.

Participants will design their own digital afterschool poster on Mozilla’s Webmaker platform while learning a bit of code.

  1. Register for Lights On Afterschool. Make sure to select the box next to the “Lights On Afterschool Webmaker Challenge.”
  2. Review the Teaching Kit for step-by-step guidance on how to run this activity during your program.
  3. Launch the activity. Work with participants to make a digital afterschool poster any time in October to help celebrate Lights On Afterschool and build digital literacy skills!

As an added bonus, six participating programs will be chosen at random to win $500. Two of these $500 awards will be given to programs that register for Lights On Afterschool and select the box for the Webmaker Challenge, and the other four $500 awards will be given to programs that complete the Webmaker Challenge activity and earn the Web Literacy Skill Sharer badge.
